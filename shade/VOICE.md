# The shade's voice

The rules every user-facing string follows. They existed implicitly from
v0.1; written down 2026-07-12 so tone survives across sessions and
contributors. When a new string doesn't fit these, fix the string — or
propose changing the rule in a PR, not silently.

## Who we're talking to

A smart friend who has used iPhones and Macs their whole life, is not
technical, and just bought a strange, calm tablet. They are never wrong;
the machine explains itself.

## The rules

1. **Familiar where familiar, novel where novel** (founder ruling
   2026-07-12). Concepts that exist on iPhone keep Apple's exact words:
   Do Not Disturb, Rotation Lock, Airplane Mode, Dark Mode, Wi-Fi,
   Bluetooth. Invented vocabulary is reserved for what is genuinely new
   on this device — the light ("pure reflective", "candlelit",
   "paper-like", "screen-like"). Never invent a word for an old thing;
   never borrow a stale word for a new thing.
2. **Sentences, not labels.** "The network list arrives with the Sol:OS
   blessing" — not "Unavailable". Settings rows read as a friend
   explaining: "Where should paper-like end? Up to here the backlight
   only improves contrast…"
3. **Lowercase calm.** Sentence case for prose; lowercase for our own
   surfaces ("essentials", "everything else…", "shade setup", "clear
   all"). Proper nouns and Apple terms keep their capitals.
4. **Honest about the blessing.** When something waits on the Sol:OS
   build, say so plainly and say what works meanwhile. Never pretend;
   never silently fail; every dead end is a hand-off with a reason.
5. **Numbers only when they change a decision.** Battery is "plenty",
   not "67%"; the percent appears only when it's time to think about a
   charger ("low · 22%"). Brightness has no visible number at all —
   words and position carry the meaning.
6. **Name the consequence, not the subsystem** — but only where rule 1
   permits. "Screen sleeps after · 10 minutes", "the page holds still".
7. **No blame, no alarm.** The crash note reads "the shade hit trouble
   a few times in a row, so it stepped aside" — the machine takes the
   fault and states the recovery.
8. **No exclamation marks, no emoji, no jargon nouns** (SSID → network;
   pairing mode → "put it in pairing mode" only when quoting the
   earbud's own manual reality).
9. **Teach once, then be quiet.** First-pull notes vanish by doing.
   Nothing nags, rotates tips, or repeats itself.
10. **Punctuation:** the interpunct (·) separates a thing from its
    state; the em-dash sets off explanations; ellipsis (…) marks doors
    to bigger rooms ("everything else…").

## Words we use

the page (the screen when reading) · the room (ambient light) · the
blessing (Sol:OS shipping us as a system app) · hand-off (opening a
system surface) · the shade (this panel) · pull (opening it).

## Words we never use

enable/disable (use on/off) · toggle (as a noun) · permission denied ·
error · invalid · manage · configure · SSID · APK (except in the club's
developer-facing docs).
