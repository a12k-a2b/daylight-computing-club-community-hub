THE SIX INSTINCTS

of the Daylight Computer Club — table manners for a potluck, not rules for a lobby

1
RECIPROCITY

keep the account open

A store settles up instantly; a gift refuses to settle. Asking, thanking, showing off, coming back — the unsettled balance is the friendship.

2
VULNERABILITY

it's safe to be an amateur here

Amateur means one who does it for love. Homemade things have fingerprints on them — nothing is too small, too weird, or too unfinished to bring.

3
OPEN RECIPES

every dish comes with the recipe

Nothing on the shelf is a black box — the source is on every card, and remixing is the highest compliment. Each app is a running start for the next.

4
HUMAN SCALE

open because we're small

Trust comes from knowing each other, not from gates. A potluck for forty is a potluck; for forty thousand, it's a food court. Grow by starting another one down the street.

5
MADE-FOR-ONE

the smallest software is the best kind

Stores carry what sells to a million people. The club exists for things worth making for one — your kid, your morning, one little annoyance.

6
UNHURRIED

daylight, not dopamine

No feeds, no streaks, nothing pings you. One newsletter a month, a demo night a quarter, a shelf that waits patiently.

a potluck, not a store
