# Comparison — Fable × Sol, QS redesign ideation (sealed, then revealed)

Both wrote independently from the same evidence packet (Sol's worktree
was snapshotted before Fable's ideas were committed).

## Convergences (independent agreement — the strong signal)

1. **Outcome language over subsystem names.** Fable's one-line state
   sentence + Sol's outcome tile labels ("Page stays upright", "Radios
   resting") are the same instinct on different surfaces. Cheapest
   highest-leverage direction both found.
2. **Teach once, kinesthetically, then vanish.** Fable's first-pull
   caption; Sol's in-panel annotations that each disappear when the user
   performs the related action (the better mechanic — dismissal by doing).
3. **Soften the hand-off cliff.** Fable reframed stock Settings as "the
   attic" + an essentials page; Sol added consequence-before-continue
   rows and a return-breadcrumb (platform-side). Same diagnosis: the
   scariest moment is leaving the shade.
4. **Receipts/undo.** Sol: say what just happened. Fable: make the scary
   one reversible. Complements, not duplicates.
5. **Discipline list.** Both independently: no timers/automation
   creeping in, no numeric percentages, no visual iOS cosplay, beware
   caption clutter.
6. **Same blind spots named:** zero user evidence (all founder taste),
   "iPhone-native" is not one skill level, copy may not be read at all
   during a 2-second pull, and the worst intimidation lives BEYOND the
   shade (grants, pairing dialogs, captive portals) where the shade
   can't follow — Sol's articulation, adopted.

## The one genuine disagreement

**Presets.** Fable: named lights (Daylight/Overcast/Candle/Moonless)
should become the *face* of light control, sliders one layer deeper —
iPhone people live in modes. Sol: deliberately NOT primary — four
unexplained recipes would *replace* learning; the dials-with-vocabulary
must teach the room/backlight model first; presets are graduation
shortcuts later. This is an ordering question, not a feature question,
and it is Anjan's call: is the shade primarily a teacher (dials first)
or an operator's panel (modes first)? Sol's position is the
conservative default since the dial vocabulary shipped this week and is
unproven on other humans.

## Ideas unique to one brain (the diversity dividend)

- Sol: light-area verdict sentence spanning BOTH dials; three human
  pairs (connection / attention / page) as the grid's structure;
  ambient-light "the room can take over" hint (honestly gated on
  platform calibration); notification empty-state as a situational
  one-line lesson.
- Fable: the essentials mini-settings page (the attic reframe made
  concrete); battery as time/words not percent; VOICE.md; pushing the
  Settings RRO up the platform-ask priority list.

## Disposition

Merged, cost-tiered list delivered to Anjan in chat 2026-07-12. No
experiment this round (ideation, fully reversible); the sealed files
stand as the record. Reopen trigger: first real novice user test of the
shade (the shared blind spot all of this rests on). Review point: before
shelving the shade on the club (v0.2 exit).

## Founder ruling (Anjan, 2026-07-12, on convergence #1)

Partially adopted, with a correction both brains missed: do NOT invent
vocabulary for concepts that aren't new. Customers arrive from
iPhone/iPad/Mac — where the concept already has an Apple name, use it
verbatim. Applied immediately: Quiet → "Do Not Disturb", Rotation →
"Rotation Lock" (Apple's inverted frame: on = page holds still),
Airplane → "Airplane Mode", Dark → "Dark Mode"; all sublabels plain
on/off. Invented vocabulary is reserved for what is genuinely novel on
this device: the light (zone legend, presets-to-be). Outcome-poetry on
familiar pills ("radios resting", "page can turn") is rejected.
